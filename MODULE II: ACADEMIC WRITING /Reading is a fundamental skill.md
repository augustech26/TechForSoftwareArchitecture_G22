Reading is a fundamental skill in the academic field, as it allows us to access knowledge and understand information in a critical manner. To approach a text effectively, it is important to consider the different levels of reading. According to Dr. Daniela Rengel Encinas, there are three levels of reading: superficial reading, comprehensive reading, and critical reading. The first consists of a first approach to the text to identify the main information, the second involves a deeper understanding of the content, and the third invites us to question and analyze the text in a reflective manner.

On the other hand, when writing an academic article, it is essential to follow a structured process consisting of several stages. The stages of academic writing include planning, writing, revising, and editing. Planning allows us to organize our ideas and arguments in a coherent manner, writing leads us to express those ideas in a clear and concise text, revising helps us to correct possible errors and improve the quality of the text, and editing allows us to polish the final article.

As for the difference between informal and formal language, it is important to highlight that informal language is characterized by its colloquial tone, its use of everyday expressions, and its lack of rigor in grammatical terms. On the other hand, formal language is characterized by its more elaborate structure, its specific vocabulary, and its grammatical correctness. In the academic field, it is essential to use formal language to ensure the seriousness and credibility of the text.

Finally, spelling and grammar play a crucial role when writing an academic article. Good spelling guarantees the clarity and coherence of the text, while proper grammar allows ideas to be conveyed accurately and correctly. Spelling or grammatical errors can affect the understanding of the text and detract from its academic quality. Therefore, it is essential to pay attention to these aspects when writing an academic article.

In conclusion, academic reading and writing are fundamental skills that require practice and dedication. By understanding reading levels, following the stages of academic writing, using formal language, and taking care of spelling and grammar, we can improve our ability to communicate ideas effectively in the academic setting.
