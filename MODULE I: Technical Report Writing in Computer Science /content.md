# Characteristics of good writing

Clarity: A well-written text must be clear and easy to understand. The message should be conveyed in a straightforward manner, avoiding unnecessary jargon or complexity. The reader should have no doubt about the purpose or meaning of the text.

Coherence: Ideas should flow logically from one to the next, with smooth transitions between sentences and paragraphs. Coherence ensures that the text makes sense as a whole and that each part supports the overall message.

Conciseness: Good writing is concise, meaning that it communicates the necessary information without unnecessary repetition or wordiness. Every word should serve a purpose, and the writer should avoid filler words or overly elaborate phrases.

Accuracy: The content should be factually correct and precise. Accurate writing reflects well on the writer and ensures that the information provided is reliable and trustworthy. This includes correct spelling, grammar, and punctuation.

Tone and Style: The tone should be appropriate for the intended audience and purpose of the text. Whether formal, informal, persuasive, or informative, the style should be consistent throughout. The choice of words and sentence structure should align with the overall tone.

Engagement: A good piece of writing captures the reader’s attention and keeps them engaged. This can be achieved through interesting facts, compelling arguments, or well-crafted narratives. Engaged readers are more likely to absorb and retain the information presented.

Organization: Well-organized writing is easy to follow. Each section of the text should have a clear purpose, with an introduction, body, and conclusion. The structure should allow the reader to easily find and understand the key points.

Grammar and Syntax: Proper use of grammar, punctuation, and sentence structure is essential. A good writer pays attention to detail, ensuring sentences are well-constructed and free of errors that might confuse or distract the reader.

Creativity: While clarity and accuracy are paramount, creativity in writing can make the text more engaging and memorable. This can include interesting word choices, analogies, metaphors, or innovative ways of presenting information.

Relevance: A good piece of writing stays focused on the topic and the needs of the audience. Irrelevant or off-topic information can distract from the main message and reduce the effectiveness of the writing.

## 🔗 Use of Accent Marks in Capital Letters
In Spanish, accent marks (tildes) are typically used on vowels to indicate stress and pronunciation. While accent marks are generally not used on capital letters in many languages, Spanish requires them on capital letters when necessary. For example, words like "TÉCNICA" or "ÁRBOL" should include accent marks on capital letters, as omitting them can change the meaning or lead to confusion.

## 🔗 What Bullet Points Are and How to Use Them (Fundéu)
Bullet points are symbols (usually dots or dashes) used to list items, making information clearer and easier to read. According to Fundéu (Fundación del Español Urgente), bullet points are commonly used in both formal and informal writing to present key points, instructions, or elements in a concise manner. They should be used sparingly and when items are of equal importance or closely related.

# How to Use Bullet Points:

Start each bullet point with a consistent structure (e.g., all points as complete sentences or fragments).
Maintain parallelism, ensuring all points are of the same grammatical form (e.g., all verbs in the same tense).
Use bullet points when it enhances clarity and structure.

## 🔗 Enumeration (Wikilengua)
Enumeration refers to the act of listing or numbering items in a sequence. According to Wikilengua, proper enumeration involves using numbers or bullet points to present items in a clear order, which helps to organize ideas logically. It is especially useful in instructional or procedural writing, where steps need to be followed in a particular order.

# Tips for Proper Enumeration:

Number the points when the order is important (e.g., steps in a process).
Use bullet points or other symbols when the order doesn't matter.
Ensure the items listed are related and relevant to the topic.

## 🔗 Spanish Language Dictionary (RAE)
The Royal Spanish Academy (RAE) provides the official dictionary for the Spanish language, ensuring that words, grammar, and spelling adhere to standardized rules. The RAE is an invaluable resource for writers, as it offers definitions, correct spelling, and guidance on usage. In formal writing, it is important to refer to the RAE for accurate and current language norms.

# Why It’s Important:

It helps ensure the correct spelling of words and usage.
It is a reliable source for finding the meaning of unfamiliar terms.
Writers should always check RAE when in doubt about a word's meaning or spelling.

## 🔗 Writing Rules for Prefixes (RAE)
Prefixes are groups of letters added at the beginning of words to modify their meaning. The RAE provides clear guidelines on how to correctly use prefixes in Spanish. For example, some prefixes are joined with a hyphen, while others are attached directly to the root word. It’s crucial to follow these rules to maintain clarity and precision in writing.

# Common Rules for Prefixes:

When adding a prefix, it should always be followed by the root word without a space (e.g., "subir" → "subiría").
Some prefixes require a hyphen when combined with proper nouns (e.g., "anti-Obama").
Prefixes should be consistent in usage and should not change the meaning of the root word unexpectedly.
