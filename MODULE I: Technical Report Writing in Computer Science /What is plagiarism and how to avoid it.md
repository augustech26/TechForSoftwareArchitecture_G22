Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's ideas, words, or work without giving proper credit, making it appear as if it’s your own. It can include copying text verbatim, paraphrasing without acknowledgment, or using someone’s research or findings without citation​

### WRITING TUTORIAL SERVICES

To avoid plagiarism:

Always cite your sources when you use another person's idea, quote, data, or even a paraphrased version of their work. This applies to text, statistics, graphs, and images.
Paraphrase carefully: When restating someone else's ideas, ensure you use your own words, and still provide proper attribution to the original source​

Use quotations: If you’re using exact words from a source, they must be enclosed in quotation marks with the source clearly cited​

Distinguish your work: Ensure that your ideas are clearly separated from those of others in your notes and draft, so you don’t accidentally mix them up​

Keep track of your sources: Especially for digital content, where it can be easy to forget where you found information. Always note down URLs, the date you accessed a website, and other citation details​
